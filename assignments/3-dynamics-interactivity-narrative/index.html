Ease of Doing Business ranking and access to electricity as of 2016

All Countries

If we plot every country's Ease of Doing Business Ranking (y-axis, ranking where 1 = most favorable) against their access to electricity (x-axis; %), it's clear there's a linear relationship.

The countries that have high access to electricity have an easier time doing business.

Top 40 Easiest Countries to Do Business

Take a look at the cluster of countries in the bottom right of this plot. The 40 best countries to do business all have 100% access to elecricity

What about Sub-Saharan Africa?

Sub-Saharan Africa

The linear relationship is highlighted here, where, in general, the more access to electricity a country has, the easier it is to do business.

Let's focus on a smaller subset

Countries in Sub-Saharan Africa with Less Than 20% Access to Elecricity

Outside of a few outliers in Rwanda, Zambia, and Uganda, all Sub-Saharan African countries with less than 20% access to electricity also fall within the 50 hardest countries to do business in.

A Closer Look

Let's take a closer look at three Sub-Saharan African countries that have some of the harshest business environments and least access to electricity:

  • South Sudan: 9% Access to Elecricity // 186 Ease of Doing Business Ranking
  • Chad: 9% Access to Elecricity // 180 Ease of Doing Business Ranking
  • Liberia: 20% Access to Elecricity // 174 Ease of Doing Business Ranking

How can these countries improve their ability to do business and their access to electricty? By taking advantage of their natural renewable energy potential.
