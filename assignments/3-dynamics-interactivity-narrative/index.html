Ease of Doing Business ranking and access to electricity as of 2016

All Countries

If we plot every country's Ease of Doing Business ranking on the y-axis (where 1 = most favorable) against their access to electricity percentage on the x-axis, it's clear there's a linear relationship.

The countries that have high access to electricity have an easier time doing business.

Top 50 Easiest Countries to Do Business

Take a look at the cluster of countries in the bottom right of this plot. The 50 best countries to do business in all have 100% access to elecricity

What about Sub-Saharan Africa?

Sub-Saharan Africa

The linear relationship is highlighted here, where, in general, the more access to electricity a country has, the easier it is to conduct business.

Let's focus on a smaller subset

Countries in Sub-Saharan Africa with Less Than 25% Access to Elecricity

All Sub-Saharan African countries with less than 25% access to electricity also fall within the 60 hardest countries to do business in, with an average ranking of just 163 out of 190.

A Closer Look

Let's take a closer look at three Sub-Saharan African countries that have some of the harshest business environments and least access to electricity:

  • Chad: 9% Access to Elecricity // 180 Ease of Doing Business Ranking
  • Niger: 16% Access to Elecricity // 150 Ease of Doing Business Ranking
  • Mozambique: 24% Access to Elecricity // 137 Ease of Doing Business Ranking

How can these countries improve their ability to do business and their access to electricty at the same time? By taking advantage of their natural renewable energy potential.
