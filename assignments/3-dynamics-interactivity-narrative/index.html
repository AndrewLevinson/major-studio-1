Section 1

What is the Ease of Doing Business Index?

.

Origin

Created at the World Bank, the ease of doing business score and subsequent ranking means the "regulatory environment is more conducive to the starting and operation of a local firm."

The rankings are determined by sorting the aggregate scores on 10 topics, each consisting of several indicators, giving equal weight to each topic.

The index takes the scores from 190 countries and ranks them in order of difficulty. A score of 1 corresponds to the easiest regulatory environment and a score of 190 is the most difficult.

    Starting a Business Dealing with Construction Permits Getting Electricity Registering Property Getting Credit Protecting Minority Investors Paying Taxes Trading across Borders Enforcing Contracts Resolving Insolvency

Why is a high score important?

Currently New Zealand holds the number 1 ranking and Somalia has harshest business environment at 190.

Section 2

Ease of Doing Business and access to electricity

.

All Countries

If we plot every country's Ease of Doing Business ranking on the y-axis (where 1 = most favorable) against their access to electricity percentage on the x-axis, it's clear there's a linear relationship.

The countries that have high access to electricity have an easier time doing business.

Top 50 Easiest Countries to Do Business

Take a look at the cluster of countries in the bottom right of this plot. The 50 best countries to do business in all have 100% access to electricity

What about Sub-Saharan Africa?

Sub-Saharan Africa

The linear relationship is highlighted here, where, in general, the more access to electricity a country has, the easier it is to conduct business.

Let's focus on a smaller subset

Countries in Sub-Saharan Africa with Less Than 25% Access to Electricity

13 Sub-Saharan African countries have less than 25% access to electricity. All of them also fall within the 30% hardest countries to do business in in the world, with an average ranking of just 163 out of 190.

So how about a business opportunity in energy? Well it just so happens that in many of these Sub-Saharan countries, there's some of the highest renewable solar and wind potential in the entire world. Africa accounts for more solar irradiance than any other continent and in many countries meets the minimum requirements for wind power.
